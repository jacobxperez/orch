## Permitted Nominative Use

You may use the **Orch** name nominatively to:

- refer to the official Orch orchestration system in accurate, factual statements
- cite Orch in educational, academic, news, commentary, or comparative works
- describe truthful compatibility with Orch, subject to the “Compatibility Claims” section below
- identify that your project integrates with Orch, when the statement is accurate and non-misleading

**Requirements:**

- Use the name only as necessary to identify Orch.
- Do not alter the spelling “Orch.”
- Do not stylize Orch as a verb.
- Do not pluralize or modify Orch in a way that implies your own brand.
- Do not use Orch as the leading distinctive element of your product, company, package, module, service, domain, social handle, or project name.
- Include a clear attribution statement when appropriate, such as:  
  “Orch™ is a trademark of Jacob Perez.”

Permitted nominative use does not imply endorsement, sponsorship, certification, commercial licensing, host approval, or affiliation.

## Compatibility Claims

You may describe truthful compatibility with Orch, provided that:

- the claim is accurate and testable
- the claim does not imply certification, endorsement, sponsorship, official status, host approval, or affiliation
- the claim does not imply that your runtime, host, platform, wrapper, integration, or module is an official Orch runtime or official Orch component
- the claim does not imply that you have commercial, host, redistribution, OEM, certification, or official approval rights
- the claim is accompanied by a clarifying line where reasonable, such as:  
  “Not affiliated with or endorsed by the Orch project.”

Examples of acceptable compatibility wording may include:

- “Compatible with Orch runtime vX.Y for evaluation use.”
- “An integration for Orch.”
- “A toolkit for Orch.”
- “Works with Orch through public host bridges.”

Examples that require written permission include:

- “Certified for Orch”
- “Official Orch”
- “Orch-approved”
- “Orch-certified”
- “Canonical Orch”
- “Orch host certified”
- “Host approved for Orch”

If you want to use “Certified for Orch,” compatibility badges, certification marks, host approval marks, or similar wording, you must obtain prior written approval.
